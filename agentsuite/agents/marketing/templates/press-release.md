## Lead Paragraph
The lead must answer: Who? What? When? Where? Why? in 50–75 words. A journalist should be able to print the lead alone and the story would still make sense.

**Goal:** {{ campaign_goal }}

> [Write the lead paragraph here. Start with the company name and the most important fact. Do not start with "Today" or "We are excited."]

---

## Quote 1 — Executive
A quote from a founder, CEO, or senior executive that provides strategic context. Should sound like a real person, not a press release.

**[Executive Name], [Title], {{ brand_name }}:**

> "[Write the quote. 2–3 sentences. State the significance of this announcement and what it means for customers or the market. Avoid adjectives like 'thrilled' and 'excited.']"

## Body Paragraph
Provide supporting detail, context, and any key facts or figures. This is where you explain the *how* and *why* behind the announcement.

[2–3 paragraphs. Each paragraph = one key point. Use specific numbers wherever possible. No vague superlatives.]

**Key metrics / proof points to include:**
- [Stat or milestone 1]
- [Stat or milestone 2]
- [Relevant market context]

**Quarter context:** {{ quarter }}

A third-party voice that validates the announcement. Should be specific and credible.

> "[Write the quote. 2–3 sentences that speak to the benefit they've experienced or why this announcement matters to the market.]"
