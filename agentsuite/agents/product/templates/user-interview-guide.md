## Warm-Up Questions

*Purpose: build rapport, understand context, set the stage.*

1. Tell me a little about your role and what a typical day looks like for you.

2. How long have you been dealing with [problem area]? What was your first experience with it?

3. Walk me through the last time you had to [task related to core problem]. What were you trying to accomplish?

---

## Core Questions

*Purpose: understand pain points, current workflow, and unmet needs.*

4. What's the hardest part of [problem area] for you right now? Can you give me a specific example?

5. Walk me through how you currently handle [specific task]. Don't skip any steps — I want to understand every tool, person, or workaround involved.

6. When [current workaround] fails or frustrates you, what do you do? Has that ever cost you time, money, or credibility?

7. Have you tried other solutions? What did you like about them? Why did you stop using them or keep using them?

8. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [problem area], what would it be?

9. Who else is affected when this problem occurs? How do they find out?

10. *(If applicable)* What would make you trust a new tool with this problem?

---

## Prototype / Demo Questions

*Use if showing a prototype or existing product.*

11. Before I explain anything — what do you think this does?

12. Walk me through what you'd do first. Think out loud.

13. What's confusing or unexpected here?

14. Where would you go if you got stuck?

15. If this existed today, would you use it? What would stop you from adopting it?

---

## Wrap-Up

16. Is there anything about [topic] that I didn't ask about that you think I should know?

17. Who else should I talk to? Would you be willing to connect me?

18. On a scale of 1–10, how much does this problem affect your work? What would make it a 10?

*Thank participant. Explain next steps and how findings will be used.*
